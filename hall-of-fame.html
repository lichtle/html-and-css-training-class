20 biggest Flamengo players

  • Pirillo (1941 to 1947)

    He arrived at Flamengo with the arduous mission of replacing the idol Leônidas da Silva. Despite the skepticism, he quickly showed his worth and became one of the attacking references of the Rio de Janeiro club. To this day, he is the highest scorer in one edition of the state championship, having scored 39 goals in 1941.

  • Sávio (1992 to 1997 and 2006)

    The blond angel of Gávea never hid his identification with Flamengo. Dressed in red and black, he was part of the squads that won the Brasileirão in 1992 and the Carioca championship in 1996.

  • Romário (1995 to 1997 and 1998 to 1999)

    Elected the best player in the world after Brazil's victory in the 1994 World Cup, Romário left Barcelona for Flamengo. Despite the hype, the 'Baixinho' (Shorty) only won his first title in 1996. Throughout the years defending the Rubro-Negro, Romário scored 204 goals.

  • Rondinelli (1971 to 1981)

    Known by the fans as the "Deus da Raça" (God of Determination), he was the hero of the 1978 state title when he scored an unforgettable header goal against Vasco.

  • Evaristo de Macedo (1953 to 1957 and 1965 to 1967)

    Idolized both at Barcelona and Real Madrid, Evaristo de Macedo is regarded as one of the great players of his generation. At Flamengo, he won the state championships in 1953, 1954, 1955, and 1965. He also managed the club on three occasions as the head coach of the first team.

  • Nunes (1980 to 1984)

    After playing for several clubs, Nunes, also known as João Danado, became the 'Decisive Scorer' wearing the Flamengo jersey, where he was one of the heroes of the Intercontinental Cup in 1981.

  • Bruno Henrique (2019 - today)

    With his speed and leaping ability that brings a smile to every Flamengo fan's face, Bruno Henrique has secured his place in history as one of the top scorers of Flamengo in the 21st century. He has been a crucial player in titles such as the 2019 Libertadores and the 2019 and 2020 Brasileirão championships, among other trophies.

  • Everton Ribeiro (2017 - today)

    A midfielder with great skill, Everton Ribeiro scored stunning goals and won important titles representing one of the most remarkable generations in Flamengo's history.

  • Dida (1954 to 1963)

    Dida was Zico's greatest idol. The 'Little Rooster from Quintino' never hid his admiration for the striker, which is why he did everything to play for Flamengo. Despite his slender build, Dida became the club's top scorer until the Zico era, scoring 257 goals in 364 matches between 1954 and 1963.

  • Adriano (2000 to 2001, 2009 to 2010 and 2012)

    The Emperor was always regarded as one of the main prospects in Flamengo's youth categories. However, when he reached the professional level, he wasn't properly utilized and soon left the club. Nonetheless, he returned to Gávea, where he became the hero of the 2009 Brasileirão triumph.

  • Petkovic (2000 to 2002 and 2009 to 2011)

    Pet, as he is also known, entered Flamengo's history initially with the free-kick goal that secured the 2001 state title against Vasco. After a turbulent departure, the Serbian accepted to return as a way to settle a debt the club had with him, despite much skepticism. Contrary to what many believed, he became a key piece in the 2009 national title.

  • Andrade (1979 to 1988)

    One of the greatest champions in Flamengo's history, Andrade was part of four Brasileirão victories with the Rubro-Negro (1980, 1982, 1983, and 1987), and also played a role in the team's triumph in the Libertadores and Intercontinental Cup. As if that wasn't enough, he was also one of the main contributors to the 2009 Brazilian title, when he took charge of a discredited squad and led them as a coach.

  • Adílio (1975 to 1987)

    Considered one of the most skillful players of his generation, Adílio shone in the triumphs of the Copa Libertadores and the Club World Cup alongside Zico, Andrade, and Júnior.

  • Leônidas da Silva (1936 to 1941)

    Regarded as the best player of the 1938 World Cup, the 'Black Diamond' is considered one of the first great idols of Brazilian football. With Flamengo, he won the 1939 Carioca championship.

  • Zizinho (1939 to 1950)

    With Zizinho, Flamengo won its first three consecutive state championships between 1942, 1943, and 1944. This was in addition to the Carioca Championship of 1939. Zizinho left Flamengo with 329 games and 146 goals, and he is considered the greatest idol of the club until the emergence of Zico.

  • Arrascaeta (2019 - today)

    One of the greatest foreigners in Flamengo's history, Arrascaeta has been synonymous with class, stunning goals, game vision, and a lot of determination on the field since 2019 until today.

  • Leandro (1979 to 1990)

    Greatest right-back in the history of Flamengo, Leandro played for the club throughout his professional career, where he won the Copa Libertadores and the Club World Cup, in addition to the Brazilian Championships in 1980, 82, 83, and 87.

  • Gabigol (2019 - today)

    Hero of the Copa Libertadores titles in 2019 and 2022 and the top scorer of Flamengo in the 21st century, Gabigol is the main idol of a new generation of Flamengo supporters who witnessed the striker's decisive performances throughout his tenure. With goals in two Copa Libertadores triumphs, Gabi enters the podium of Flamengo's greatest.

  • Junior (1974 to 1984 and 1989 to 1993)

    The great partner of Zico in Flamengo's greatest triumphs in history. Despite being right-footed, he established himself as a left-back in the Brazilian national team in 1982. After a relatively unremarkable spell in Italy, he returned to the Rio de Janeiro club to become the team's leader as a midfielder. Throughout his career, he wore the red and black jersey in 865 matches, becoming the player who represented Flamengo the most times.

  • Zico (1971 to 1983 and 1985 to 1989)

    Known as the "God of Gávea," Zico is a unanimous figure among Flamengo supporters. As the main driving force behind the Copa Libertadores and Intercontinental Club titles in 1981, he has garnered a legion of fans who idolize him to this day.
